Sports & Hobbies

How I spend my free time.

Snowboarding
Winter Season

As soon as the first real snow drops I'm looking at trail maps. I grew up snowboarding and it's still the thing I look forward to most every year. New England mountains are underrated — and when the season is good, there's nothing better than getting out there with friends on a powder day.

⛳
Golf
Summer Season

Golf took over my summers completely. There's something uniquely frustrating and addictive about it — every round is different and there's always something to work on. Boston has great public courses and I try to get out as often as I can from May through October. Always down for a round if you're in the area.

🏒
Hockey
Year-Round · Men's League

I play in a men's league with a group of friends — it's one of the best parts of my week. Late-night ice times, cold rinks, and a postgame that usually runs longer than the game itself. Hockey is one of those things that keeps you connected to people in a way most other activities don't.

Home Base

Why I chose to stay in Boston.

I came to Boston for school five years ago and somewhere along the way it stopped feeling like a college city and started feeling like home. That doesn't happen everywhere — a lot of people leave the second they graduate. I'm not one of them.

Boston has a density of smart, ambitious people that's hard to find outside of a few cities in the country. The tech, finance, and healthcare scenes are genuinely world-class. For someone coming out of a program like Northeastern's, it's an ideal place to build a career — especially in analytics and data.

But beyond the professional side, the city just fits my life. The seasons are real, the sports culture is unlike anywhere else, and you can get to mountains or the coast in under two hours. After five years, I know this place — the neighborhoods, the rhythms, the people. It makes sense to stay.
