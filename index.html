About Me

Analyst by training,
generalist by nature.

I'm a graduating senior at Northeastern University studying Business Analytics and Finance with a minor in Data Science. My path into analytics started in high school through sports — tracking stats, looking for patterns, trying to understand why teams won or lost beyond what the box score showed. That early curiosity turned into a discipline, and eventually a career direction.

What drives me is the gap between data and decisions. Numbers alone don't change anything — someone has to translate what the data is saying into something leadership can act on. That's the skill I've been building across three co-ops: at AMETEK as a Sales Analyst, at Bevi as a Supply Chain Analyst, and most recently at WHOOP as a Product Analyst. In every role, the technical work only mattered when it was communicated clearly to the people making the calls.

I've lived in Boston for five years and plan to stay. When I'm not working with data you'll find me on a golf course, a hockey rink, or a mountain somewhere.
